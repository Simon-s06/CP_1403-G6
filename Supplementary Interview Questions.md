**Supplementary Interview Questions**

1\. Workload and Team Recovery

* What happened: Besides the overtime case before the investor presentation, how many work interruptions or errors caused by high-intensity overtime occurred in the past 6 months? What were the core causes of each overtime (urgent customer demands, process loopholes, staff shortages, etc.)?
* Why: When you noticed employees showing signs of overwork, what methods did you try to adjust workloads (temporary outsourcing, adjusting delivery cycles, pausing non-urgent tasks)? Why did these methods fail or work poorly?
* How do you feel: What was your first reaction when you saw the team needed 1-2 weeks to recover from overtime? Did this affect your confidence in the team's long-term sustainable operation?



2\. Cash Flow and Labor Costs

* What happened: How many times was salary payment delayed due to slow customer payments in the past year? What was the length of each delay and how many employees were affected?
* Why: What percentage of total labor costs come from statutory benefits (EPF, SOCSO, paid leave)? Have you tried to optimize these costs? What stopped you from implementing these changes?
* How do you feel: What was your state of mind when you faced salary payment risks and had to communicate with employees? Were you worried about losing staff and hurting team stability?



3\. Multi-role Pressure and Decision Dilemmas

* What happened: What specific work omissions or decision errors did you make in the past 3 months due to wearing multiple roles? (e.g., payroll mistakes, failing to resolve team conflicts in time)
* Why: Have you tried to outsource HR work (attendance, payroll) or hire part-time staff to share the load? What stopped you from doing this (cost concerns, trust issues, process complexity)?
* How do you feel: After making key decisions (hiring freezes, task reallocation) with incomplete information, do you keep worrying about the results? How does this anxiety affect your daily work?



4\. Employee Retention and Team Communication

* What happened: How many employees resigned in the past year due to salary, overtime, or work pressure? Did they share their core concerns with you before leaving?
* Why: What non-salary methods did you try to attract and retain staff? Why didn't these methods work as expected?
* How do you feel: How did you feel when employees left because the company couldn't offer competitive salaries? Do you think honest communication about the company's situation is not enough to ease employees' dissatisfaction?



5\. Balancing Compliance and Costs

* What happened: Have you ever faced compliance risks (regulatory reminders, employee complaints) due to inaccurate calculation of EPF/SOCSO benefits? What exactly happened?
* Why: Why do you always prioritize compliance when it conflicts with cost control? Have you ever thought about "simplifying compliance temporarily to cut costs"? Why didn't you do this in the end?
* How do you feel: Do you feel helpless facing the dilemma that "compliance raises costs, but cost control risks breaking rules"? Does this conflict add to your mental burden?
