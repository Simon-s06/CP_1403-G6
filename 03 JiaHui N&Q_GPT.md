# Jia Hui Persona Analysis

## Needs

| Needs | Interview Evidence |
|---|---|
| Hope to attract and retain employees within a limited budget | She could not offer salaries comparable to larger companies but still wanted employees to feel secure and valued. |
| Need more efficient HR management tools | She handles attendance, scheduling, leave records, and payroll tasks regularly. |
| Need better workload distribution mechanisms | When employees struggle with their workload, she has to adjust deadlines and redistribute tasks. |
| Want greater transparency in team communication | She prefers listening first and being honest about what the company can and cannot provide. |
| Need to maintain employees' physical and mental well-being | She wants to avoid long periods of overtime that lead to exhaustion and burnout. |
| Need stable cash flow to support operations | She hopes to avoid freezing hiring or reducing manpower due to financial pressure. |
| Need to balance legal compliance with cost control | She has to consider EPF, SOCSO, paid leave, and other employment obligations. |
| Want to reduce her own psychological burden | She carries significant anxiety while trying to remain calm for her team. |

---

## Pain Points

| Pain Points | Evidence from the Interview |
|---|---|
| Long working hours cause physical and mental exhaustion | She worked until after midnight for nearly four consecutive nights before an investor demo. |
| Limited recruitment budget | The startup could not provide competitive salaries compared with larger companies. |
| Actual manpower costs are higher than expected | Besides salaries, EPF, SOCSO, paid leave, and other benefits increase expenses. |
| Unstable cash flow | Delayed client payments may affect the company's ability to pay staff properly. |
| Insufficient manpower | Hiring freezes force existing employees to take on additional responsibilities. |
| Employee burnout | The team needed one to two weeks to recover after periods of intense overtime. |
| Declining work quality | Fatigue led to reduced performance and increased mistakes. |
| Increased team conflict | Small misunderstandings escalated into larger arguments during stressful periods. |
| Multiple roles and responsibilities | She acts as a founder, HR manager, mediator, and payroll administrator simultaneously. |
| High uncertainty in decision-making | Many decisions are made with incomplete information. |
| Hidden personal anxiety | She suppresses her own stress to maintain confidence for the team. |

---

## Core Problem Statement

> **As a small startup founder, Jia Hui struggles to balance employee well-being, legal employment obligations, and limited financial resources while ensuring the business remains sustainable and productive.**

**Chinese Translation:**

> 作为一家小型创业公司的创始人，Jia Hui 需要在员工福祉、劳动法规要求和有限资金之间取得平衡，同时确保企业能够持续运营并保持团队效率。

---

## User Requirements

| User Requirements |
|---|
| The system should help founders monitor employee workload and redistribute tasks efficiently. |
| The system should automate payroll calculations, including EPF and SOCSO contributions. |
| The system should track leave, attendance, and work schedules in one place. |
| The system should alert managers when employees show signs of excessive overtime. |
| The system should provide visibility into manpower costs and cash flow impacts. |
| The system should support transparent communication regarding employee benefits and policies. |
| The system should generate reports to assist hiring and staffing decisions. |

---

## Summary

### Jia Hui's Core Needs

- Manage the team without causing employee burnout.
- Control manpower costs within a limited budget.
- Simplify time-consuming HR administrative tasks.
- Reduce decision-making pressure and personal anxiety.

### Jia Hui's Biggest Challenges

- **Not enough money (cash flow)**
- **Not enough people (manpower)**
- **Not enough time (overtime)**
- **Too much pressure (anxiety)**

These interconnected issues form the major pain points Jia Hui faces as the founder of a small startup.
